Ropa Vieja

Background

Ropa vieja is a traditional Cuban dish that is made with shredded beef, vegetables, and spices. The name "ropa vieja" translates to "old clothes" in Spanish, which is a reference to the shredded appearance of the beef. This dish is typically served with rice, black beans, and plantains, and is a popular meal in many Cuban households.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs flank steak
  • 1 large red onion, sliced
  • 1 large green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 large red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 1/2 cup broth
  • 1/4 cup dry red wine
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 2 packs of sazon

Instructions

  1. Season the flank steak with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the flank steak and sear on both sides until browned, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  4. Remove the steak from the pot and set aside.
  5. Add the onions, bell peppers, and garlic to the pot and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  6. Return the steak to the pot and add the tomato sauce, broth, wine, cumin, oregano, paprika, salt, pepper, bay leaves, and sazon.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and shreds easily.
  8. Remove the steak from the pot and shred it with two forks.
  9. Return the shredded meat to the pot and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes.
  10. Remove the bay leaves before serving.
  11. Serve the ropa vieja with rice, black beans, and plantains.
