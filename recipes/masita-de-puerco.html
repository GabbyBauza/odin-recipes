Masita de Puerco

Background

Masita de puerco is a traditional Cuban dish that is made with fried pork chunks, garlic, and spices. The name "masita de puerco" translates to "little pork" in Spanish, which is a reference to the small size of the pork chunks. This dish is typically served with rice, black beans, and plantains, and is a popular meal in many Cuban households.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup sour orange juice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water

Instructions

  1. Season the pork with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the pork and sear on all sides until browned, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the pork from the pot and set aside.
  5. Add the garlic, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper to the pot and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  6. Return the pork to the pot and add the sour orange juice, lime juice, sugar, and water.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 2-3 hours, or until the pork is tender.
  8. Serve the masita de puerco with rice, black beans, and plantains.
